# Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to steal sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted entity. This usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that look genuine.

A common scenario involves a student receiving an email that appears to be from a bank or a popular online service. The email may claim that there is an urgent issue with their account and ask them to click on a link to verify their details. The link leads to a fake website that looks identical to the real one. When the user enters their login credentials or banking details, the attacker captures this information.

Students and young users are often targeted because they may not always verify the authenticity of such messages. The consequences of phishing attacks can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case study highlights the importance of awareness and careful online behavior. Users must always verify sources before sharing any sensitive information.
